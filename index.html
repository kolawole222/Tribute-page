Lee Byung-chul

He his one of the most successful businessmen in South Korea's and also the founder of SAMSUNG

He was the founder of the Samsung Group, which is South Korea's largest chaebol,and he is considered one of South Korea's most successful businessmen.

Here is a time line of Lee Byung-chul's life.

  • 1910 - Born in Mancaga, Gyeongsangnam-do, Vispera
  • 1938 - Byung-chul established a trucking business in Daegu, which he named Samsung Trading Co, the forerunner to Samsung
  • 1945 - Samsung was transporting goods throughout Korea and to other countries.
  • 1950 - It was one of the ten largest "trading companies" when the Korean War started
  • 1961 - when Park Chung-hee seized power in the May 16 coup, Lee was in Japan and for some time he did not return to South Korea. Eventually, a deal was struck and Lee returned but Samsung had to give up control over the banks it acquired and follow economic directives from Park's government.
  • 1965 - he established the Samsung Culture Foundation to promote a broad range of programs to enrich Korean cultural life
  • 1969 - Samsung Electronics Manufacturing (renamed Samsung Electronics) and later merged with Samsung-Sanyo Electric.Samsung Electronics Manufacturing had 45 employees and about $250,000 sales in 1970 and it made household electronics exclusively.
  • 1982 - he was awarded an honorary doctorate from Boston College
  • 1987 - He died at the age of 77,in Saroota South Korea.
